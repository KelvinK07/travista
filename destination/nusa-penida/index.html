About Highlight Location Review

Nusa Penida is a picturesque island that can be found in close proximity to the southeastern coast of the Indonesian island of Bali. It is not just an island of natural beauty but also serves as a district within the larger administrative framework of the Klungkung Regency. This district, Nusa Penida, encompasses not only the main island but also the neighboring small gem, Nusa Lembongan, and an array of twelve even smaller islands, each with its own unique charm and character.

The captivating Nusa Penida is nestled within the embrace of the Badung Strait, a body of water that gracefully separates this island from its more renowned and bustling neighbor, Bali. The Badung Strait, with its crystal-clear waters and marine life, serves as a natural border between the tranquil Nusa Penida and the vibrant, culturally rich Bali, creating a distinct sense of place for both destinations. This geographical division accentuates the unique appeal of Nusa Penida, making it a sanctuary for those seeking a more serene and pristine island experience.
